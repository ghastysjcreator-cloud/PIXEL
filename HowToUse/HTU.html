            🖌️ How To Use PIXEL

Welcome to PIXEL, a simple and fun pixel-art editor where you can create artwork one pixel at a time! Follow this guide to learn how all the tools work.

🎨 Getting Started
Click the Draw button on the homepage.
The editor will open with a large pixel grid in the center of the screen.
Every square on the grid represents one pixel.
Click any square to color it using your currently selected color.
🌈 Choosing Colors

The color palette is located near the top of the editor.

To select a color:
Click on any color in the palette.
The selected color becomes your active drawing color.
Click on the grid to place pixels using that color.

You can switch colors at any time while drawing.

✏️ Drawing Pixels

Drawing is simple:

Select a color.
Click a square on the grid.
The square will change to your selected color.
Continue placing pixels to create your artwork.

Pixel art is created one square at a time, allowing for precise control and detailed designs.

🪣 Fill All Tool

The Fill All button quickly fills the entire canvas with the currently selected color.

To use Fill All:
Choose a color from the palette.
Click Fill All.
Every pixel on the canvas will instantly change to that color.

⚠️ Be careful when using this tool, as it affects the entire canvas.

🧹 Clear Canvas

The Clear button removes all colors from the canvas.

To clear your artwork:
Click Clear.
The grid will return to a blank state.
You can immediately start drawing again.

This is useful when beginning a new project.

💾 Saving Your Artwork

When your masterpiece is finished:

Click Save Image.
PIXEL will generate an image from your drawing.
Save the file to your device.

This allows you to keep your artwork, share it with friends, or upload it elsewhere.

🎯 Tips For Better Pixel Art
Start Small

Simple designs are often the easiest way to learn pixel art.

Use Limited Colors

Many great pixel-art creations use only a few colors.

Zoom In Mentally

Think of each square as an important part of the final image.

Plan Your Outline

Creating an outline first can make larger drawings easier.

Experiment

Don't be afraid to try different colors, shapes, and styles.
